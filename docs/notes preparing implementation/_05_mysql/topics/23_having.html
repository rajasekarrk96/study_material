Having

The HAVING clause is used to filter grouped data (results of GROUP BY). It works after aggregation, unlike WHERE.

Basic Concept & Syntax

🔹 Note
HAVING is used to filter groups, not rows. It is used when we want to apply a condition based on aggregate results.

🔹 Syntax
SELECT column_name, aggregate_function(column_name)\nFROM table_name\nGROUP BY column_name\nHAVING condition;

Example 1: Departments with total salary > 90000

🔹 Goal
Show departments where the group's total salary exceeds 90,000.

🔹 Example
SELECT department, SUM(salary) AS total_salary\nFROM employees\nGROUP BY department\nHAVING SUM(salary) > 90000;
Result:
departmenttotal_salary
IT110000

Example 2: Departments with more than 1 employee

🔹 Goal
Show departments that have more than one employee recorded.

🔹 Example
SELECT department, COUNT(emp_id) AS emp_count\nFROM employees\nGROUP BY department\nHAVING COUNT(emp_id) > 1;
Result:
departmentemp_count

Practice Worksheet

Q1. Count students by age and display only ages having more than 1 student.
Query:
SELECT age, COUNT(*) AS student_count
FROM students
GROUP BY age
HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;

Output:
+------+---------------+
| age  | student_count |
+------+---------------+
| 23   | 2             |
+------+---------------+
Q2. Display age groups having exactly 1 student.
Query:
SELECT age, COUNT(*) AS student_count
FROM students
GROUP BY age
HAVING COUNT(*) = 1;

Output:
+------+---------------+
| age  | student_count |
+------+---------------+
| 22   | 1             |
| 24   | 1             |
+------+---------------+
Q3. Display sum of ages by age and show only sums greater than 22.
Query:
SELECT age, SUM(age) AS total_age
FROM students
GROUP BY age
HAVING SUM(age) > 22;

Output:
+------+-----------+
| age  | total_age |
+------+-----------+
| 23   | 46        |
| 24   | 24        |
+------+-----------+
Q4. Display ages where count of students is less than 3.
Query:
SELECT age, COUNT(*) AS student_count
FROM students
GROUP BY age
HAVING COUNT(*) < 3;

Output:
+------+---------------+
| age  | student_count |
+------+---------------+
| 22   | 1             |
| 23   | 2             |
| 24   | 1             |
+------+---------------+
Q5. Display ages where count of students is between 1 and 2.
Query:
SELECT age, COUNT(*) AS student_count
FROM students
GROUP BY age
HAVING COUNT(*) BETWEEN 1 AND 2;

Output:
+------+---------------+
| age  | student_count |
+------+---------------+
| 22   | 1             |
| 23   | 2             |
| 24   | 1             |
+------+---------------+
